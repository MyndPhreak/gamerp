# Government & Budget System

## Overview

The government collects tax revenue and spends it on public services, salaries, and infrastructure. Unlike the Federal Reserve, the government cannot create moneyâ€”it must balance revenue with spending.

## Government Structure

### Leadership Models

**Option 1: Elected Officials**
- Players vote for Mayor/President
- Term length: 2-4 weeks (real-time)
- Mayor controls budget allocation
- Can be impeached for mismanagement

**Option 2: Admin-Controlled**
- Server admins manage government
- More stable, less drama
- Good for smaller servers
- Can transition to elected later

**Option 3: Hybrid**
- Admins set constraints
- Elected officials manage within bounds
- Best of both worlds

### Government Roles

**Mayor/President**
- Sets budget priorities
- Signs/vetoes legislation
- Appoints department heads
- Represents government publicly

**Treasurer**
- Manages government accounts
- Processes payments
- Reports financial status
- Ensures balanced budget

**Department Heads**
- Police Chief
- Fire Chief
- EMS Director
- Public Works Director

## Budget Process

### Revenue Sources

**Tax Revenue (Primary):**
- Income tax (largest source)
- Sales tax
- Property tax
- Business license fees
- Vehicle registration
- Fines and penalties

**Other Revenue:**
- Mining license auctions
- Land sales
- Government-owned businesses
- Federal Reserve loans (debt)

**Example Weekly Revenue:**
```
Income tax: $500,000
Sales tax: $150,000
Property tax: $100,000
Business licenses: $50,000
Vehicle registration: $20,000
Fines: $30,000
--------------------------
Total: $850,000/week
```

### Expenditures

**Mandatory Spending (Cannot Cut):**

**Public Safety (40%):**
- Police salaries: $200,000/week
- EMS salaries: $100,000/week
- Fire department: $80,000/week
- Equipment: $20,000/week

**Administration (15%):**
- Mayor salary: $50,000/week
- Government staff: $75,000/week
- Office expenses: $25,000/week

**Infrastructure (10%):**
- Road maintenance: $50,000/week
- Public buildings: $30,000/week

**Deposit Insurance Fund (10%):**
- Bank insurance premiums: $85,000/week

**Discretionary Spending (25%):**
- Public works projects
- Subsidies
- Welfare programs
- Special events
- Economic development

**Example Weekly Budget:**
```
REVENUE: $850,000

EXPENDITURES:
Mandatory: $640,000 (75%)
Discretionary: $210,000 (25%)
--------------------------
Total: $850,000

SURPLUS/DEFICIT: $0 (balanced)
```

### Balanced Budget Requirement

**Rules:**
- Government cannot spend more than revenue over time
- Can run deficits short-term (borrow from Federal Reserve)
- Must repay loans with interest
- Persistent deficits require tax increases or spending cuts

**Deficit Spending:**
```
Week 1: Revenue $800,000, Spending $900,000
Deficit: $100,000
Action: Borrow from Federal Reserve at 5% interest

Week 2-4: Must repay $105,000 over time
Options:
1. Increase taxes
2. Cut discretionary spending
3. Delay non-essential projects
```

### Budget Transparency

**Public Dashboard:**
- Current treasury balance
- Weekly revenue (by source)
- Weekly spending (by category)
- Outstanding debt
- Budget vs. actual
- Historical trends

**Reports:**
- Weekly financial summary
- Monthly budget report
- Quarterly economic outlook
- Annual state of the economy

## Government Salaries

### Public Sector Jobs

**Police Officers:**
- Salary: $1,500/hour
- Source: Government budget
- Requirements: Pass training, clean record
- Capacity: Based on budget (e.g., 10 officers max)

**EMS/Paramedics:**
- Salary: $1,000/hour
- Source: Government budget
- Requirements: Medical training
- Capacity: Based on budget

**Firefighters:**
- Salary: $1,200/hour
- Source: Government budget
- Requirements: Fire training
- Capacity: Based on budget

**Judges:**
- Salary: $2,000/hour (or per case)
- Source: Government budget
- Requirements: Legal knowledge, appointment
- Capacity: 2-3 judges

**City Workers:**
- Salary: $800/hour
- Source: Government budget
- Jobs: Road repair, maintenance, cleanup
- Capacity: Variable

**Government Staff:**
- Salary: $1,000/hour
- Jobs: DMV clerk, tax office, city hall
- Capacity: Based on need

### Salary Mechanics

**Payment:**
- Paid hourly while on duty
- Automatically deducted from government budget
- Income tax automatically withheld
- Requires clocking in/out

**Budget Constraints:**
- If budget is insufficient, salaries reduced or delayed
- Government can freeze hiring
- Can lay off workers during crisis
- Players notified of budget issues

**Example:**
```
Government budget: $300,000
Police on duty: 5 officers @ $1,500/hour
EMS on duty: 3 medics @ $1,000/hour
Total hourly cost: $10,500/hour

Budget depletes in: 28.5 hours
Action needed: Reduce staff, increase taxes, or cut other spending
```

## Government Contracts

### Public Works Contracts

**Process:**
```
1. Government identifies need (e.g., build new road)
2. Request for Proposals (RFP) issued
3. Players/businesses submit bids
4. Government awards contract to lowest/best bid
5. Contractor completes work
6. Government inspects and pays upon completion
```

**Example Contracts:**
- Build police station expansion: $200,000
- Pave new roads: $50,000
- Install traffic lights: $20,000
- Construct public park: $100,000

**Benefits:**
- Creates jobs
- Circulates money to private sector
- Improves infrastructure
- Drives economic activity

### Service Contracts

**Ongoing Services:**
- Trash collection: $10,000/week
- Security for government buildings: $15,000/week
- IT services: $5,000/week
- Legal services: Variable

## Government Borrowing

### Borrowing from Federal Reserve

**When Needed:**
- Budget deficit
- Emergency spending (disaster, crisis)
- Large capital projects
- Economic stimulus

**Terms:**
- Interest rate: 3-5% (set by Federal Reserve)
- Repayment period: 4-12 weeks
- Automatic payment from tax revenue
- Limits on total debt (e.g., max 50% of annual revenue)

**Example:**
```
Government borrows: $1,000,000
Interest rate: 5%
Term: 10 weeks
Weekly payment: $105,000

Must ensure budget has $105,000/week for 10 weeks
```

### Bonds (Advanced)

**Government Bonds:**
- Government sells bonds to players
- Players pay cash now, get cash + interest later
- Example: $10,000 bond, 8% interest, 8 weeks
- Player receives $10,800 after 8 weeks

**Benefits:**
- Raises money without Federal Reserve
- Players get safe investment
- Lower interest than Federal Reserve loans
- Builds community investment

## Economic Crises

### Budget Crisis

**Causes:**
- Tax revenue drops (recession, population decline)
- Overspending
- Corruption/mismanagement
- Economic shock

**Effects:**
- Salary delays or cuts
- Service reductions
- Layoffs
- Asset sales

**Solutions:**
- Emergency tax increase
- Spending cuts
- Federal Reserve loan
- Austerity measures

### Banking Crisis

**Deposit Insurance Fund:**
- Funded by bank insurance premiums
- Government guarantees deposits up to $50,000
- If bank fails, fund pays depositors

**If Fund Insufficient:**
- Government must borrow to cover
- Can lead to government crisis
- May require bailout (controversial)

## Economic Policy Tools

### Fiscal Policy

**Expansionary (Stimulate Economy):**
- Increase government spending
- Cut taxes
- Public works projects
- Hire more workers

**Contractionary (Cool Economy):**
- Decrease spending
- Raise taxes
- Pay down debt
- Reduce workforce

### Subsidies & Incentives

**Industry Subsidies:**
- Mining subsidy: $100 per gold bar (encourage money supply)
- Farming subsidy: Support food production
- Manufacturing: Support key industries

**Tax Incentives:**
- New business tax breaks
- Hiring credits
- Investment incentives

  expenditures: integer

  debt: integer

GovernmentEmployee {
  id: unique_id

  position: enum

}

  id: unique_id
  title: string

  budget: integer

  completion_date: timestamp
}

  id: unique_id
  amount: integer
  interest_rate: float
  term_weeks: integer
  weekly_payment: integer
  remaining_balance: integer
}
