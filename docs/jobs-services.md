# Jobs & Services Economy

## Overview

The economy is driven by players working jobs and providing services to each other. Jobs are divided into government-funded (public sector) and player-funded (private sector).

## Public Sector Jobs (Government-Funded)

### Police Officer

**Salary:** $1,500/hour (from government budget)

**Responsibilities:**
- Enforce laws
- Respond to emergency calls
- Issue traffic citations
- Arrest criminals
- Patrol city

**Requirements:**
- Clean criminal record
- Complete training course
- Government application approval

**Revenue for Government:**
- Ticket fines
- Asset seizures from criminals
- Property seizures

### EMS/Paramedic

**Salary:** $1,000/hour (from government budget)

**Responsibilities:**
- Respond to medical emergencies
- Revive downed players
- Transport to hospital
- Provide medical treatment

**Requirements:**
- Medical training course
- Government approval

**Revenue:**
- Players can be charged for services ($500 per revive)
- Insurance reimbursements

### Firefighter

**Salary:** $1,200/hour (from government budget)

**Responsibilities:**
- Respond to fires
- Extinguish fires
- Rescue trapped players
- Prevent fire spread
- Fire safety inspections

**Requirements:**
- Fire training course
- Physical fitness

**Revenue:**
- Fire safety inspection fees (businesses)
- Insurance reimbursements

### Judge

**Salary:** $2,000/hour or $500 per case

**Responsibilities:**
- Preside over court cases
- Issue verdicts
- Set bail amounts
- Sentence convicted players
- Interpret laws

**Requirements:**
- Legal knowledge
- Appointment by government
- Neutral reputation

### City Worker

**Salary:** $800/hour (from government budget)

**Responsibilities:**
- Repair roads
- Clean streets
- Maintain public property
- Assist with public projects

**Requirements:**
- Government employment application

### Government Clerk

**Salary:** $1,000/hour

**Positions:**
- DMV clerk (vehicle registration)
- Tax office clerk
- City hall receptionist
- Permit office clerk

**Responsibilities:**
- Process applications
- Assist players with government services
- Maintain records

## Private Sector Jobs (Player-Funded)

### Miner

**Pay:** Variable (sell gold to Federal Reserve)

**Description:**
- Extract gold from mines
- Process gold ore
- Sell to Federal Reserve for currency
- **Critical job**: Only way new money enters economy

**Revenue:**
- ~$60,000-90,000/hour (realistic)
- Can form mining companies and hire employees

**Requirements:**
- Mining license ($1,000)
- Mining equipment

### Banker

**Pay:** Business owner (profit) or salary (employee)

**Description:**
- Accept deposits
- Issue loans
- Manage bank operations
- Provide financial services

**Owner Revenue:**
- Interest rate spread (loan interest - deposit interest)
- Service fees
- ATM fees

**Employee Salary:**
- $800-1,500/hour (set by bank owner)

**Requirements:**
- Banking license ($10,000)
- Initial capital ($100,000)
- Financial knowledge

### Shop Owner

**Pay:** Profit from sales

**Business Types:**
- Gun shop
- Clothing store
- Electronics shop
- Furniture store
- Pawn shop

**Revenue:**
- Buy goods from wholesaler (NPC or player)
- Mark up price
- Sell to players
- Keep profit

**Example:**
```
Buy gun from wholesaler: $2,000
Sell to player: $5,000
Profit: $3,000
```

**Requirements:**
- Business license ($5,000/month)
- Storefront rental
- Initial inventory investment

### Restaurant Owner

**Pay:** Profit from food sales

**Description:**
- Cook food
- Serve customers
- Can hire cooks and waiters

**Revenue:**
- Sell meals to players
- Profit margin: 50-70%

**Costs:**
- Ingredients
- Employee salaries
- Rent
- Business license ($7,500/month)

### Gun Dealer

**Pay:** Profit from weapon sales

**Description:**
- Sell firearms and ammunition
- Can be player-owned or NPC

**Revenue:**
- High-margin business
- Weapons: 40-60% markup
- Ammo: 30-50% markup

**Requirements:**
- Gun dealer license ($12,000/month)
- Background check
- Secure storage

**Risk:**
- Liable if used in crime (optional realism)

### Vehicle Dealer

**Pay:** Profit from vehicle sales

**Description:**
- Sell new and used vehicles
- Can offer financing (loans)
- Trade-ins

**Revenue:**
- New vehicles: 15-25% markup
- Used vehicles: 20-40% markup
- Financing interest

**Requirements:**
- Dealer license ($10,000/month)
- Lot space
- Initial inventory

### Mechanic

**Pay:** Service fees from players

**Description:**
- Repair damaged vehicles
- Customize vehicles
- Upgrade performance

**Revenue:**
- Repair fees: $500-5,000 per job
- Customization: $1,000-10,000

**Requirements:**
- Mechanic skill/training
- Tools and garage space

### Security/Bodyguard

**Pay:** Contract fees from players/businesses

**Description:**
- Protect VIPs
- Guard businesses

- Prevent robberies

**Revenue:**
- Hourly rate: $1,000-3,000

**Requirements:**
- Combat skills
- Reputation

### Taxi/Transport Driver

**Pay:** Fares from passengers

**Description:**

**Description:**

### Insurance Agent

**Types:**

### Construction Worker

**Description:**

- Private construction

**Revenue:**
- Employee: $1,000-1,500/hour

## Service Economy

### Player-to-Player Services

Players pay each other for services, creating money circulation:

**Examples:**
- Mechanic fixes car: Player pays $2,000
- Lawyer defends in court: Player pays $10,000
- Security guards business: Business pays $3,000/hour
- Doctor provides checkup: Player pays $500
- Taxi drives across city: Player pays $300

**Money Flow:**
```
Player A earns $100,000 from mining
→ Pays mechanic (Player B) $2,000 for repairs
→ Player B spends $500 at restaurant (Player C)
→ Player C pays rent $1,000 to landlord (Player D)
→ Money circulates through economy
```

## Employment System

### Hiring Employees

**Business Owner Process:**
```
1. Post job listing (in-game job board)
2. Set salary ($800-1,500/hour)
3. Interview applicants
4. Hire player
5. Employee clocks in/out
6. Owner pays salary (automatic deduction)
7. Owner pays payroll taxes
```

**Benefits for Owners:**
- Scale business beyond personal time
- Employees generate revenue
- Profit = Revenue - (Salaries + Expenses)

**Benefits for Employees:**
- Steady income
- Don't need startup capital
- Learn business skills
- Lower risk than ownership

### Example: Restaurant Employment

**Owner (Player A):**
- Owns restaurant
- Hires cook (Player B) @ $1,000/hour
- Hires waiter (Player C) @ $800/hour
- Restaurant earns $5,000/hour in revenue
- Costs: $1,800/hour in salaries
- Profit: $3,200/hour for owner

**Employees:**
- Cook earns steady $1,000/hour
- Waiter earns $800/hour + tips
- No risk, no capital needed

## Job Balancing

### Preventing Job Imbalance

**Too Many Miners (Inflation):**
- Decrease gold value
- Increase mining difficulty
- Add mining quotas
- Increase mining license fees

**Too Few Miners (Deflation):**
- Increase gold value
- Decrease mining difficulty
- Offer mining subsidies
- Reduce license fees

**Too Many Police:**
- Government budget limits slots
- Only pay for X police officers
- Rest go on reserve/unpaid

**Too Few Essential Services:**
- Government bonuses
- Subsidies
- Recruitment campaigns

### Income Balancing

**Goal:** All jobs should be viable

**Earning Targets (per hour):**
- Entry-level: $30,000-50,000
- Mid-level: $50,000-100,000
- High-level: $100,000-200,000
- Specialized: $200,000+

**Adjust:**
- Salaries
- Service fees
- Profit margins
- Demand for services

## Unemployment & Welfare

### Unemployment Benefits

**Eligibility:**
- No job for 1+ week
- Previously employed
- Not fired for cause

**Benefits:**
- $500/week basic income
- Max 4 weeks
- Must job search

**Funding:**
- Government budget (discretionary)
- Unemployment tax on employers (optional)

**Purpose:**
- Prevent player frustration
- Encourage continued play
- Temporary safety net

### Job Training Programs

**Government-Funded:**
- Police academy (free)
- Medical training (free)
- Fire training (free)

**Private:**
- Mechanic certification ($5,000)
- Real estate license ($3,000)
- Law degree ($10,000)

**Benefits:**
- Higher-paying jobs
- Specialization
- Career progression

## Integration with Economy

### Money Circulation

**Cycle:**
```
1. Miner creates money (sells gold)
2. Miner spends on services (mechanic, food)
3. Service providers spend on goods (shops)
4. Shop owners pay employees
5. Employees spend on services
6. Government collects taxes at each step
7. Government pays public sector salaries
8. Public sector spends back into economy
```

### Economic Multiplier

**Example:**
```
Miner earns $100,000
→ Spends $20,000 at mechanic
→ Mechanic spends $10,000 at restaurant
→ Restaurant owner spends $5,000 at shop
→ Shop owner spends $2,000 on security

Total economic activity: $137,000 from initial $100,000
```

## Implementation Notes

### Database Schema
```
Job {
  id: unique_id
  title: string
  type: public|private
  salary_rate: integer (if public)
  employer_id: player_id (if private)
  requirements: json
}

Employment {
  id: unique_id
  job_id: foreign_key
  employee_id: player_id
  employer_id: player_id (null if government)
  salary_rate: integer
  hire_date: timestamp
  status: active|terminated
}

TimeLog {
  id: unique_id
  employee_id: player_id
  clock_in: timestamp
  clock_out: timestamp
  hours_worked: float
  pay_earned: integer
}
```

### Key Functions
```
HireEmployee(employer, employee, salary)
ClockIn(employee)
ClockOut(employee)
PaySalary(employment_id)
OfferService(provider, customer, service, price)
CompleteService(transaction_id)
RateService(transaction_id, rating)
```

### Events
- `OnHired`: Player hired for job
- `OnFired`: Employment terminated
- `OnClockIn`: Work shift started
- `OnClockOut`: Work shift ended
- `OnSalaryPaid`: Employee received pay
- `OnServiceCompleted`: Service transaction finished
